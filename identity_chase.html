Identity Chase

Objective

To create role-playing video game with a 2D, pixel art style

Background

For my CISC 298 Project I and CISC 498 Project II courses at Harrisburg University of Science and Technology, I collaborated with an Interactive Media major for two semesters on developing a role-playing video game using GameMaker Studio 2. I wanted to create a video game for our independent research project because I wanted to explore my interest in video game development. Video game development would allow me to combine my love of programming with my creative nature. For Project I, my partner and I collaborated on researching video game engines and video game design and development. We also worked together on designing the various aspects of our game such as the storyline, characters, and levels. For Project II, we worked on our respective tasks to complete the project.

My Role

I worked with my partner on designing the game such as creating a storyline, planning levels and objects, and choosing the aesthetic. I was responsible for back-end development, so I programmed different features such as keyboard controls, player interactions with NPCs, and game mechanics in GameMaker Language, GML. I also wrote the dialogue for the NPCs.

Personal Goals

  • To learn how to use a game engine
  • To learn about the video game development process
  • To explore my interest a different field of computer science
  • To create a project that I can continue after class
